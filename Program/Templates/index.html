The human face is a very complex and distinctive part of the body. In fact, it is one of the most complex signaling systems available to us; it includes more than 40 structurally and functionally independent muscles, each of which can be activated independently of the others; and it forms one of the strongest indicators of emotions.

When we laugh or cry, we display our emotions, allowing others to glimpse our minds as they "read" our faces based on changes in key facial components, such as the eyes, eyebrows, eyelids, nose, and lips.
